Research

Ledgerback Digital Commons Research Cooperative ("Ledgerback-DCRC" or "DCRC") is an citizen-owned, independent non-profit research and education cooperative. The DCRC is a hub for distributed community-powered learning and research networks ("flocks") of researchers, activists, learners, academicians, and ambassadors from diverse backgrounds dedicated to the anti/inter/multidiscplinary study of the network society and moderating and structuring/innervating the transformation towards a global technological commonwealth in the network society. Any motivated person – regardless of background or origin – is welcome at the DCRC. The network society is a society overwhelming shaped by the cheap access and easily distrbutable of information via interconnected networks of machines (e.g., the Internet).

Advisory Council

The members at the DCRC are always looking for advisors to come aid us in our activities and general running of the cooperative. We expect an advisor to aid us in one or more of the following ways:

  1. Advice and assistance in conducting our activities
  2. Provide mentorship to our members
  3. Help DCRC seek funding to conduct our activities
  4. Help DCRC connect to interested stakeholders and movements
If you would like to be an advisor, please send an email to ledgerback@gmail.com.

Mission

The DCRC is motiviated to develop global community research and learning networks to unify the multifaceted understandings and views of the network society in order to identify, diagnosis and address societal, scientific and technical issues that may arise from, or can be cured by, technology, to structure and coordinate the change-relevant actors and movement into a socoiotechnical imaginary, as well as developing and supporting innovative free and open-source tools, training and events.
